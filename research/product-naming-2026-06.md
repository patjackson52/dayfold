## VERDICT

**The family-organizer / smart-calendar naming space is saturated.** Every
evocative single word (home / hearth / kin / day / brief / calm) already maps
to a live competitor, and no exact single-word `.com` is available. The
viable path is a **coined/compound name + accept brand ≠ exact `.com`**.

**Recommended name: `Dayfold`.** It survived three rounds of adversarial
screening with **no hard blocker at any layer** — trademark clear, app-store
clear, coined and protectable. Cost of entry is a modifier domain
(`joindayfold.com`) and moving before an emerging "Dayfold AI" footprint
hardens.

**Confidence: MEDIUM-HIGH.** Two residual operator actions close the gap
(authenticated USPTO/EUIPO read; lock domain + file ITU). See
[§5](#5-residual-operator-work).

## 1. Method

Three rounds:

1. **Round 1** — generated ~16 on-theme candidates; screened each for
   competitor collision + USPTO trademark + `.com` ownership via fan-out
   agents and `whois`.
2. **Round 2** — fresh batch of 10 more-distinctive coined/compound names
   after round 1 showed the obvious words are taken.
3. **Round 3** — deep-dive on the lead survivor (`Dayfold`) across four
   angles: USPTO/intl trademark, app/product/package collision, company
   entity + social handles + linguistics, and a hard attempt to defeat the
   register paywalls/JS-gates.

**Tooling caveat (applies throughout):** USPTO `tmsearch.uspto.gov` and
`tsdr.uspto.gov` are JS apps; EUIPO/WIPO are JS/CAPTCHA-gated; the mirrors
(Justia, Trademarkia, Trademarkelite, uspto.report) 403/404 automated fetch.
Trademark conclusions rest on **search-engine + aggregator snippets**, with
the method validated by control queries that correctly surfaced real records
(`DATAFOLD`, `DAILY FOLD`, `FOLD`, `JUSTIA`). This is strong screening, **not
an authenticated clearance** — that residual needs a logged-in human session
or paid service (Corsearch / counsel).
