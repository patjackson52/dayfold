Timeline — an authored hub property
Dayfold · extends the Hubs detail anatomy · light + dark · Compose-named

The spine of a hub

Hubs already hold a flat list of blocks. A timeline gives a hub an axis of time — so a months-long project and a single frantic day are both legible at a glance. One authored property, rendered at two scales: a day timeline for a busy day, and a hub roadmap spanning weeks or months. Each lives as a calm card in the dossier and taps open to a full, linkable view.

Card + detail Two scales Light + dark Authored, with provenance
01Two scales, one timeline

The layout signals the span

A day reads top-to-bottom as a vertical rail with a live NOW line; a roadmap reads left-to-right as a horizontal spine of month milestones. Same data model, two renderings — you never have to read a label to know which scale you're looking at. In a real hub the dossier shows one card, scale auto-selected from the stops; the second scale is one tap away via the in-detail toggle. (Both are shown together here only to document the two renderings.)

Decisions baked in

Every open call a timeline raises, resolved to a single design. The timeline is a new authored, content-blind hub property (ADR 0045) — the author writes the stops; the client lays them out in time on-device. Extends the Hubs detail anatomy, the Now deep-link transform, and the two-way write model (ADR 0038/0039); per-member visibility follows ADR 0030.
