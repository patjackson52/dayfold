The to-do tap, and the fold-away

Tap a row → it fills with a check overshoot + a left-to-right strike wipe (≤200ms) + one haptic tick. Then a single burst-end debounce (~2s after your last tap) folds every freshly-checked row together — one calm motion into the collapsed “▸ N done” line, never a stagger of separate animations. The done line sorts newest-first by completion time and, past ~20, collapses to a count only. A remote change uses the same ≤200ms transition with a quiet byline, and the layout shift is deferred until your touch ends — never a glitch, never a modal.

When two people touch the same row

Never move a row out from under a finger: apply the remote state immediately, but defer the layout shift / fold until the touch ends. The race-loser reconciles with a byline — never a “your change was discarded” dialog.
