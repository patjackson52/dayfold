Authoring into a hub, on commit

Members add to hubs they can already see through three tiny editors — a markdown note, a to-do builder, a link. They never create hubs, and each block has a single writer at a time (no concurrent rich-text co-edit). Nothing saves per keystroke; it commits on Save, then enters the calm sync ladder (P1). And where a member can't author, there is simply no “+” — an emptiness that has to look deliberate, not broken.

When you can't author — the deliberate absence

A member without write access doesn't see a greyed-out, locked “+”. They see a calm, complete, read-only hub — the “+” is simply not there. The emptiness is intentional, mirroring the enrichment principle that a failure is invisible.
