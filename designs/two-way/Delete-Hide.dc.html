Two ways to make something go away

Delete is the one destructive op where a calm warn is correct — it removes the thing for the whole family, so it asks once (a sheet, not a red alert), and only if you're allowed. Hide is personal and reversible — it tucks something away for you while everyone else still sees it. The two must never feel like the same gesture.

deleteDelete · W4 · family-wide
Destructive · confirmed once · ACL-gated · “removes it for everyone.” The one place a warn is on-brand.
visibility_offHide · W5 · personal
Reversible · no confirm · always allowed · “hidden for you.” A fold, never a deletion or a censor.

Hide — personal, reversible, unmistakable

Swipe a card left — it folds into the calm “Hidden for you” section, no confirm. The more_vert overflow menu offers the same Hide as the explicit, keyboard- and TalkBack-reachable fallback. Flip Show hidden to bring them back. The copy never says “deleted” or “removed,” only “hidden for you,” and each hidden item carries a quiet “You hid this” self-reminder — there is deliberately no family-visible signal.

How Hide is triggered

One primary gesture, one accessibility fallback — the same action, two reachable doors. Swipe is the on-brand lean because it mirrors the fold; the overflow menu guarantees Hide is keyboard- and screen-reader-reachable. The long-press “Hide + Delete together” idea is dropped — it conflates a personal op with a destructive one.
